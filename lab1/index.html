Manifesto · 01

Travel, as we
understand it.

We do not chase summits. We do not stack base camps. Sagarmāthā exists for the traveler who would rather walk one valley slowly than fly over ten. We move at the pace of acclimatization, drink tea at the same teahouse twice, and pass every mani wall to the left. The mountains are not going anywhere. Neither, for a while, are we.

Principle 01
Slowness
Minimum fourteen days on the trail. Acclimatization is not optional. The mountain sets the pace, not the itinerary.
Principle 02
Reverence
Mani walls passed to the left. Chortens circled clockwise. No drones over monasteries. The sacred is not a content opportunity.
Principle 03
Smallness
Twelve trekkers per year. Six routes. One curated brief at a time. We refuse more than we accept.

Featured Route · 03
29.18°N · Forbidden Kingdom

MUSTANG LO MANTHANG

Field Note · 18 min read

"The wind does not stop. The king does not leave. The caves remember everyone who has slept in them."

— Tashi Gurung, photographer, Sagarmāthā Field Team

A walled city of eight hundred souls at 3,840 metres, where the last king of Mustang still receives visitors in a courtyard his family has occupied since 1380. The route in takes twelve days on foot, crossing four passes above 4,000 m, and ends at a red ochre wall with a single wooden gate.

Our brief runs twelve nights in June, when the barley is green and the wind — which the locals call the breath of the ancestors — drops for an hour each morning. We stay in family homes, eat buckwheat roti, and walk the caves above the city with a monk from the Chhoede Gompa.

Two photographers per departure. Six travelers total. One guide who was born inside the wall.

The Process · 06

How a brief
is built.

Phase 01
Acclimatize
A photographer and writer arrive two weeks early. They drink tea. They walk slowly. They learn the altitude before the brief is written.
3 weeks
Phase 02
Listen
We meet the teahouse owners, the lamas, the yak herders. We ask which season they would walk in, and we listen. The route is shaped around their answer.
6 weeks
Phase 03
Brief
Six travelers receive a 64-page printed brief. Hand-stitched in Patan. Includes a phrasebook, a contour map, and the name of every teahouse.
4 weeks
Phase 04
Walk
Eighteen to twenty-two days on the trail. One guide, one cook, porters as the route demands. No phones between breakfast and the evening meal.
18–22 nights
